How Each System Is Financed and Delivered

🇺🇸 United States

The U.S. relies on a fragmented multi-payer system combining employer-sponsored private insurance, Medicare for seniors, Medicaid for low-income populations, and the ACA marketplace. Approximately 8% of the population remains uninsured. High administrative costs, price variation, and lack of universal coverage contribute to the spending gap relative to peer nations. Primary care investment is lower than most OECD peers, with spending concentrated on acute and specialty care.

Your Analysis

1. Spending and Outcomes Gap

Key concept
Your task Look at the spending data side by side. The U.S. spends significantly more per person than your selected country. Yet life expectancy and infant mortality tell a different story. In your own words, what does this gap tell you about the relationship between spending and outcomes?

2. Access and Coverage

Key concept
Your task Compare how each system approaches access to care. Who is covered, how do people get in, and what barriers exist? Think about your own health policy topic. Does your population face access barriers that this country's system addresses differently?

3. The Distinctive Feature You Would Adopt

Senator letter content
Your task Every country has at least one policy feature or structural choice that helps explain its outcomes. Look at the distinctive feature highlighted in the financing section above. If you could advocate for one feature from this country to be adopted or adapted in the U.S., what would it be and why? This answer belongs in your senator letter conclusion.

4. Systems Thinking Check

Critical thinking
Your task Researchers who study health systems know that changing one element always affects others. This is called systems thinking. If the U.S. adopted the feature you identified above, what else would need to change? Think about financing, provider incentives, insurance industry, political feasibility, and patient behavior.
