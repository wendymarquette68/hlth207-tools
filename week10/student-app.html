Your savings are nearly gone.

You have reached the Medicaid spend-down threshold.

What just happened?

In most states, you must reduce your countable assets to $2,000 or less before Medicaid will pay for long-term care. You have spent down your savings to that threshold.

What Medicaid will cover from here:

✓ Nursing home care — with no day limit
✓ Some home health services (varies by state)
✓ Some community services (at risk from 2025 cuts)

What you gave up to get here: Your retirement savings. Your emergency fund. Possibly your home equity. This is called spend-down — and it is the financial reality for most middle-class families who need long-term care.
