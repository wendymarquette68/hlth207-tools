Key Terms and SDOH Domains, Public and Population Health

SDOH Domain 1: Economic StabilityIncome, employment, food security, and housing stability. Poverty directly shapes health through stress, diet, housing conditions, and access to care.
SDOH Domain 2: Education Access and QualityEarly childhood education, high school graduation, higher education, language and literacy. Educational attainment predicts income, employment, and health literacy.
SDOH Domain 3: Health and Health CareAccess to healthcare, health insurance coverage, availability of providers, quality of care, and health literacy. Many people face barriers before they ever see a provider.
SDOH Domain 4: Neighborhood and Built EnvironmentHousing quality, access to healthy foods, transportation, environmental conditions, and safety from violence. Where you live shapes how healthy you can be.
SDOH Domain 5: Social and Community ContextSocial cohesion, support systems, discrimination, incarceration, and community engagement. Structural racism and social isolation are measurable health determinants.
Population HealthAn approach that focuses on health outcomes of a group, the distribution of outcomes within the group, and the factors that shape distribution, including SDOH.
Public Health 3.0A model calling for cross-sector partnerships, upstream interventions, and community-level action to address social determinants rather than primarily treating downstream outcomes.
Healthy People 2030HHS's 10-year national health objectives initiative (2020 to 2030). Key goals include eliminating health disparities, achieving health equity, and addressing social determinants of health.
Core Functions of Public HealthThree functions defined by the IOM: Assessment (collecting data on population health), Assurance (ensuring health services are in place), and Policy Development (guiding evidence-based policy).
Essential Public Health ServicesTen services flowing from the 3 core functions. Range from monitoring health status to evaluating programs. Used as a framework for public health organization and workforce planning.
Health EquityThe state in which everyone has a fair and just opportunity to attain their highest level of health. Requires removing obstacles such as poverty, discrimination, and lack of access to good jobs.
Systems Perspective in Public HealthThe recognition that health problems are embedded in complex, interconnected systems. Effective responses address multiple system levels simultaneously rather than single causes.
Learning Objectives 1, 2, 8, 9

Where does health really come from?

The conditions where people are born, grow, live, work, and age have a greater influence on health than medical care alone. These social determinants of health explain why ZIP code is often a better predictor of health than genetic code.

2026 Policy Context: What Has Changed

CDC Lost Approximately 25% of Its Workforce in 2025: Through DOGE-directed reductions, layoffs, and voluntary early retirement offers, roughly one in four CDC employees was gone by the end of 2025, totaling more than 3,000 workers including over 1,000 scientists, physicians, and public health specialists. Programs eliminated include the Office of Minority Health, the childhood lead poisoning prevention division, and the Epidemic Intelligence Service officer program.

$11.4 Billion Clawed Back from States in March 2025: HHS rescinded $11.4 billion in public health grants to states and localities, including funds for epidemiology and laboratory capacity, vaccination programs, and community health worker initiatives. About 80% of CDC's domestic budget normally flows to states, so federal cuts are directly local cuts.

FY 2026 Budget Would Cut CDC by 53%: The administration's proposed FY 2026 budget would reduce CDC funding by more than half compared to FY 2024. Over 100 public health programs would be eliminated. These cuts directly affect the programs that address each of the five SDOH domains covered in this activity. Source: Trust for America's Health, September 2025.
