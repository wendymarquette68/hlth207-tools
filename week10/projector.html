Scenario 1

The Fall

You fall at home and break your hip. Surgery goes well. After 5 days in the hospital, your doctor orders daily physical therapy and wound care for at least 3 weeks before you can return home safely.

Students: What would you do? Make your selection in the app.

Instructor Note
Correct: Skilled Nursing Facility

Medicare covers this because of the qualifying 5-day hospital stay. Days 1–20: fully covered. Days 21–100: ~$204/day co-pay. After day 100: $0. Key teaching moment: This is one of the few times Medicare genuinely helps with LTC — but only because a hospital stay preceded it.

Scenario 2

Your Caregiver Is Gone

The person helping you at home is no longer available. You now need help with bathing and dressing every single day. You cannot safely manage alone.

Students: What would you do? Make your selection in the app.

Instructor Note — The Key Lesson
Correct: Home Health or ALF — YOU pay

Medicare does NOT cover custodial care. Bathing and dressing is not a "skilled" service. Cost to students: ~$19,449 out of pocket for 3 months. Card C ($12,000 savings) is already in trouble. 4 in 10 Americans believe Medicare is the main nursing home payer. It is not.

Scenario 3

Moving to Assisted Living

Your doctor says it is no longer safe to live with minimal support. You need a residential care environment with daily ADL help — but not 24-hour skilled nursing.

Students: What would you do? Make your selection in the app.

Instructor Note
Correct: Assisted Living — YOU pay

ALF is clinically appropriate — not SNF. But Medicaid does NOT consistently cover ALF. Cost: $64,200/year out of pocket. Card A ($85K) is nearly depleted. Ask: "How long can YOUR Life Card afford $5,350/month?" Let students do the math aloud.

Scenario 4

Memory Care

Your cognitive function has declined significantly. You have been diagnosed with moderate-to-severe dementia and are wandering at night. Your family can no longer safely keep you at home.

Students: What would you do? Make your selection in the app.

Instructor Note
Correct: Memory Care Unit — YOU pay

Standard ALF is not safe — no secured environment. Memory care averages $7,500/month nationally. As of 2025, 44% of all ALF residents have Alzheimer's or dementia. By this point most cards are at or near Medicaid eligibility. Ask: "Who got the Medicaid alert on their phone?"

Scenario 5

A Terminal Diagnosis

Your condition has progressed and curative treatment is no longer feasible. Your prognosis is 4 to 6 months. Your family asks about end-of-life care focused on comfort and dignity.

Students: What would you do? Make your selection in the app.

Instructor Note
Correct: Hospice — Medicare covers fully

Medicare fully covers ALL hospice services once a physician certifies a prognosis of 6 months or less and patient chooses comfort over cure. Nursing, medications, social work, chaplains, bereavement support for family for a year after death — all covered. Ask: "How does this change how you think about end-of-life planning?"

Scenario 6 — Current Events

The 2025 Policy Card

It is July 2025. The One Big Beautiful Bill Act has cut Medicaid by $940 billion. Your state is eliminating Home and Community-Based Services (HCBS). Federal law requires nursing home coverage to continue. Home care is being cut.

Students: How does this affect YOUR situation? Make your selection.

Instructor Note — The Equity Moment

The correct answer is "depends on savings." HCBS are OPTIONAL under federal law — states cut them first. Nursing homes are mandatory. Ask: "Stand up if your Life Card still had savings above $50,000 at this point. Those are the people who still have a choice. Everyone else may now only have one option: a nursing home." Bridge to advocacy: "This is exactly why your Senator letter matters."
