Scenario 1
The Fall

You fall at home and break your hip. Surgery goes well. After 5 days in the hospital, your doctor orders daily physical therapy and wound care for at least 3 weeks before you can return home safely.

Students: What would you do? Make your selection in the app.
📋 Instructor Note
Correct: Skilled Nursing Facility

Medicare covers this because of the qualifying 5-day hospital stay. Days 1–20: fully covered. Days 21–100: ~$204/day co-pay. After day 100: $0 from Medicare.

Key teaching moment: This is one of the few times Medicare genuinely helps with LTC — but only because a hospital stay preceded it. Without that stay, even skilled PT at home would be out of pocket.

Scenario 2
Your Caregiver Is Gone

The person helping you at home is no longer available. You now need help with bathing and dressing every single day. You cannot safely manage alone.

Students: What would you do? Make your selection in the app.
📋 Instructor Note — THE KEY LESSON
Correct: Home Health or ALF — YOU pay

Medicare does NOT cover custodial care. Bathing and dressing is not a "skilled" service. This catches families completely off guard.

Cost to students: ~$19,449 out of pocket for 3 months. Card C ($12,000 savings) is already in trouble. Ask: "Who had Medicare cover this? Who was surprised?"

4 in 10 Americans believe Medicare is the main nursing home payer. It is not. This is the moment that misconception dies.

Scenario 3
Moving to Assisted Living

Your doctor says it is no longer safe to live with minimal support. You need a residential care environment with daily ADL help — but not 24-hour skilled nursing.

Students: What would you do? Make your selection in the app.
📋 Instructor Note
Correct: Assisted Living — YOU pay

ALF is clinically appropriate — not SNF. But Medicaid does NOT consistently cover ALF. States must cover nursing home care; ALF coverage is optional and patchy.

Cost: $64,200/year out of pocket. At this point, Card A ($85K) is nearly depleted. Card C may have already hit Medicaid. Card F is barely affected.

Discussion: "How long can YOUR Life Card afford $5,350/month?" Let students do the math aloud.

Scenario 4
Memory Care

Your cognitive function has declined significantly. You have been diagnosed with moderate-to-severe dementia and are wandering at night. Your family can no longer safely keep you at home.

Students: What would you do? Make your selection in the app.
📋 Instructor Note
Correct: Memory Care Unit — YOU pay

Standard ALF is not safe — no secured environment, no dementia-trained staff. Memory care averages $7,500/month nationally ($90,000/year).

As of 2025, 44% of all ALF residents have Alzheimer's or dementia — this is the largest single population in LTC.

By this point, most cards are at or near Medicaid eligibility except D and F. Ask: "Who got the Medicaid alert on their phone?" This is a powerful class moment.

Scenario 5
A Terminal Diagnosis

Your condition has progressed and curative treatment is no longer feasible. Your prognosis is 4 to 6 months. Your family asks about end-of-life care focused on comfort and dignity — not aggressive treatment.

Students: What would you do? Make your selection in the app.
📋 Instructor Note
Correct: Hospice — Medicare covers fully

Medicare fully covers ALL hospice services once a physician certifies ≤6-month prognosis and patient chooses comfort over cure. Nursing, medications, social work, chaplains, bereavement support for family for a year after death — all covered.

Hospice can be at home, in ALF, SNF, or dedicated hospice facility. This is the most comprehensive Medicare benefit most people never know about. Ask: "How does this change how you think about end-of-life planning?"

Scenario 6 — Current Events
The 2025 Policy Card

It is July 2025. The One Big Beautiful Bill Act has cut Medicaid by $940 billion. Your state is eliminating Home and Community-Based Services (HCBS) to close a budget gap. Federal law requires nursing home coverage to continue. Home care is being cut.

Students: How does this affect YOUR situation? Make your selection.
📋 Instructor Note — THE EQUITY MOMENT

The correct answer is "depends on savings." HCBS are OPTIONAL under federal law — states cut them first when money is short. Nursing homes are mandatory.

Ask the class: "Stand up if your Life Card had savings above $50,000 when you reached this scenario. Those are the people who still have a choice. Everyone else — your only option may now be a nursing home."

Bridge to advocacy: "This is exactly why your Senator letter matters. The answer to 'who has options?' should not be 'whoever has money.'"
